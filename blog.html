Origami is the traditional Japanese art of paper folding, where a single sheet of paper is transformed into intricate designs without cutting or using glue. It emphasizes precision, creativity, and the beauty of simplicity. Below you will find a map of the popularity of Origami throughout all of Asia.






Here you will find a step by step process on how to make things in 3D Origami!

  1. Folding the Individual Pieces
  2. Assemble the Pieces
  3. Final Product





Folding the Individual Pieces

This step is the most time consuming part of the process. First, you want to start with a rectangle piece of paper. I like to cut a standard sheet of paper into 64 of these rectangles. Folding the modular pieces is simple and can be learned through this image.





Assembling the Pieces

This step is where your creation starts to take shape. By interlocking each of the pieces in a certain way, your 3D creations starts looking like how it is supposed to. To start, insert the triangular points into the bottom flaps of another piece. Then just repeat this process over and over again. I like to glue the pieces together so they do not come apart easily.





Final Product

There are really only 2 steps to making your own 3D origami. This last step is just admiring the final artwork!
